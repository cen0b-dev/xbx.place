What Are Xbox 360 ROMs?

Xbox 360 ROMs are digital images or files that contain an exact copy of the data from an original Xbox 360 game disc or digital download. These ROM or ISO files replicate the complete game data as stored on physical media, allowing players to preserve, back up, or emulate their favorite titles on modern systems.

When used with an emulator such as Xenia, Xbox 360 ROMs enable users to experience classic titles without needing the original console — while maintaining the same gameplay, visuals, and content found on authentic hardware.

Supported Formats

ISO — Disc Image

ISO files are full disc images of Xbox 360 game discs. They contain the complete filesystem and are the most common format for Redump-verified dumps. Xenia mounts ISO files directly.

XEX — Extracted Executable

XEX files are extracted game executables from the Xbox 360 filesystem. They are commonly found in Internet Archive preservation sets and load faster than full ISO images in some cases.

GOD — Games on Demand

GOD format files come from Xbox Live digital distribution. They are packaged differently from disc dumps but contain the same game content.

All formats are typically distributed as .7z or .zip archives and can be extracted with 7-Zip.
